[2] The elusive cyclotron line in 4U 1901+03: hidden, yet present
Authors: Elena Ambrosi, Antonino D' Aì, Giancarlo Cusumano, Carlo Ferrigno, Ekaterina Sokolova-Lapa, Dimitrios K. Maniadakis, Antonio Tutone, Georgios Vasilopulous, Peter Kretschmar, Christian Malacaria, Fabio Pintore
Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.13107v1
Methodology: [Observation] (Energy band: X-ray; Key Targets/Sample: 4U 1901+03)
Core Results: A combined spectral-timing analysis of NuSTAR data reveals a persistent cyclotron resonant scattering feature at an average centroid energy of E_cyc ≈ 32 keV in the X-ray pulsar 4U 1901+03, varying by only 1.6% across luminosities. The line depth anti-correlates with luminosity, while significant pulse profile evolution in the 30-40 keV range points to a drastic change in emission geometry and accretion flow structure at lower accretion rates.

[5] A low mass and radius neutron star candidate in XTE J1810-189?
Authors: Shoutao Ban, Helei Liu, Zhaosheng Li, Yupeng Chen, Guoliang Lü, Akira Dohi, Tomoshi Takeda, Hongbin Fan, Chunhua Zhu, Renxin Xu
Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12962v1
Methodology: [Observation] (Energy band: X-ray; Key Targets/Sample: XTE J1810-189)
Core Results: Analysis of a photosphere radius expansion burst from the neutron star low-mass X-ray binary XTE J1810-189 suggests a compact object with a mass < 1.3 M☉ and radius < 8 km under a high-metallicity atmosphere assumption. The direct cooling tail method yields a broad range of possible solutions, from 0.3 to 2.9 M☉, depending on composition, with the data favoring a high-metallicity envelope.

[6] MAXI J1820+070: A rapidly spinning black hole with mild disk truncation in the soft state and a warm corona
Authors: Th. V. Papavasileiou, T. S. Kosmas, O. Kosmas, I. Sinatkas
Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12950v1
Methodology: [Observation] (Energy band: X-ray; Key Targets/Sample: MAXI J1820+070)
Core Results: Broadband NuSTAR spectral modeling during the soft state indicates the black hole in MAXI J1820+070 is rapidly spinning (a > 0.75) and reveals a modest disk truncation with an inner radius up to ~3.5 Rg. The soft X-ray excess is well-described by a blackbody component (kT ≈ 0.5 keV), cooler than the inner disk, suggesting its origin in a warm corona layer beyond 10 Rg, analogous to AGN warm Comptonization models.

[9] A Census of Active Galactic Nuclei Identified by the Mid-infrared [Ne v] Line at z≤0.025
Authors: S. Yassir, A. Annuar
Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12804v1
Methodology: [Observation] (Energy band: Mid-infrared; Key Targets/Sample: 103 local (z ≤ 0.025) AGN from the IDEOS database)
Core Results: A mid-infrared [Ne v] emission line census identifies 103 local AGN, constituting ~18% of the galaxy population and revealing a population missed by other methods (~15% undetected in optical, hard X-ray, and IR continuum diagnostics). The sample has mean properties log(L_bol/erg s⁻¹)=44.5±0.7, log(M_BH/M☉)=7.3±0.6, and λ_Edd=0.15±0.11, indicating lower-mass black holes accreting at higher Eddington ratios compared to hard X-ray selected AGN, analogous to the rapidly growing SMBH population at cosmic noon.

[13] Relativistic astrospheres of gamma-ray binaries: modeling of non-thermal processes
Authors: A. M. Bykov, A. E. Petrov, K. P. Levenfish
Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12464v1
Methodology: [Simulation / Theory] (Relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations and modeling of non-thermal processes)
Core Results: 2D/3D rMHD simulations of colliding winds in gamma-ray binaries show that a Gauss-range magnetic field in the stellar wind creates elongated relativistic outflows and magnetic cocoons conducive to particle acceleration beyond PeV energies. The model predicts that such systems, hosting powerful pulsars or accreting black holes, can be bright sources of synchrotron MeV photons and multi-PeV γ-rays, consistent with recent detections from microquasars like Cyg X-3.

[15] Microquasar remnants as reservoirs of PeV cosmic rays
Authors: Leandro Abaroa, Gustavo E. Romero, Valentí Bosch-Ramón
Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12337v1
Methodology: [Theory / Modeling] (Time-dependent model for jet-cocoon system and cosmic-ray evolution)
Core Results: A time-dependent model proposes that fossil cocoons inflated by microquasar jets during their active phase can act as long-lived reservoirs of PeV cosmic rays. After the central engine shuts off, these relic cosmic rays interact with nearby dense gas, producing delayed γ-ray emission via pp collisions, potentially explaining some unidentified ultra-high-energy sources detected by LHAASO.

[16] A Hot DOG Forged in FIRE: Nuclear and Starburst Spectral Decomposition of a Luminous Infrared Galaxy Simulation with a Resolved Dust Torus
Authors: Jaeden Bardati, Philip F. Hopkins, Claude-André Faucher-Giguère
Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12328v1
Methodology: [Simulation] (Cosmological galaxy simulation with resolved AGN dust torus)
Core Results: A high-resolution cosmological simulation of a z~4.4 starburst galaxy with a super-Eddington SMBH produces a spectral energy distribution matching observed Hot Dust-Obscured Galaxies (Hot DOGs). The characteristic near- to mid-IR cutoff arises from strong absorption in the dense nuclear ISM, not the torus itself, while the flat IR spectrum is powered by AGN-heated warm dust and starburst-heated cold dust, representing a transitional phase before AGN feedback clears the nucleus.
