[1] Black hole superradiance in Poincaré gauge theory

  • Authors: Sebastian Bahamonde, Jorge Gigante Valcarcel
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19140v1
  • Methodology: [Theory]
  • Core Results: The inclusion of torsion in space-time geometry within Poincaré gauge theory allows for energy extraction from rotating black holes by Dirac fermions via chiral asymmetry. This mechanism operates while strictly preserving the Pauli exclusion principle, representing a significant departure from classical General Relativity predictions regarding superradiant scattering.

[2] Searching for dark matter X-ray lines from the Large Magellanic Cloud with eROSITA

  • Authors: Jorge Terol Calvo, Marco Taoso, Andrea Caputo, Michela Negro, Marco Regis
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19109v1
  • Methodology: [Observation] - Energy band: X-ray (1-9 keV) - Key Targets/Sample: Large Magellanic Cloud (LMC) halo
  • Core Results: Analysis of the eROSITA-DE DR1 dataset reveals no evidence for monochromatic X-ray lines from sterile neutrino or axion-like particle (ALP) decay in the LMC halo. New constraints are established for DM masses between 2 and 18 keV, providing the most stringent limits to date on the active-sterile neutrino mixing angle sin²(2θ) and ALP-photon coupling gaγ for masses below 5 keV.

[5] G objects as Primordial Black Hole-Neutron Star Remnants: Population Modeling and Multi-Wavelength Observables

  • Authors: David Morales-Zapien, Stefano Profumo ★(Famous Scholar)
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19083v1
  • Methodology: [Theory / Simulation]
  • Core Results: G objects orbiting Sgr A* are modeled as remnants of neutron stars converted into low-mass black holes via primordial black hole capture. This framework simultaneously explains the observed dynamical stability of G objects and the long-standing "missing pulsar" problem in the Galactic Center, offering a dark matter density probe on sub-parsec scales.

[8] GWTC-4.0: Tests of General Relativity. III. Tests of the Remnants

  • Authors: The LIGO Scientific Collaboration, the Virgo Collaboration, the KAGRA Collaboration, et al.
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19021v1
  • Methodology: [Observation] - Energy band: Gravitational Waves - Key Targets/Sample: 42 events from O4a (GWTC-4.0)
  • Core Results: Analysis of coalescence remnants shows overall consistency with the Kerr black hole quasinormal mode spectrum predicted by General Relativity. No evidence for post-merger echoes is found; the GR prediction lies within the 98.6% credible region for the combined sample, with any apparent deviations likely attributable to finite catalog variance.

[9] GWTC-4.0: Tests of General Relativity. II. Parameterized Tests

  • Authors: The LIGO Scientific Collaboration, the Virgo Collaboration, the KAGRA Collaboration, et al.
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19020v1
  • Methodology: [Observation] - Energy band: Gravitational Waves - Key Targets/Sample: 91 confident signals from GWTC-4.0
  • Core Results: Stringent constraints on deviations from post-Newtonian (PN) coefficients are improved by factors of 1.2-5.5. The analysis establishes a 90% credibility upper limit on the graviton mass at mg ≤ 1.92 × 10⁻²³ eV/c², confirming that the propagation and strong-field dynamics of compact binaries remain consistent with Kerr geometry.

[10] GWTC-4.0: Tests of General Relativity. I. Overview and General Tests

  • Authors: The LIGO Scientific Collaboration, the Virgo Collaboration, the KAGRA Collaboration, et al.
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19019v1
  • Methodology: [Observation] - Energy band: Gravitational Waves - Key Targets/Sample: 91 events (42 from O4a)
  • Core Results: General consistency tests find that residuals are compatible with detector noise and that final mass/spin inferences from low- and high-frequency waveform components are mutually consistent. No significant evidence is found for subdominant GW multipole moment deviations or non-GR modes of polarization, reinforcing the robustness of the vacuum GR framework.

[11] GRB 241030A: a bright afterglow challenging forward shock emission

  • Authors: J.-G. Ducoin, C. Pellouin, et al., P. A. Evans ★(Famous Scholar)
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18956v1
  • Methodology: [Observation] - Energy band: Gamma-ray, X-ray, UV, Optical - Key Targets/Sample: GRB 241030A (z = 1.411)
  • Core Results: The bright afterglow modeling suggests a jet with extreme kinetic energy and a very low prompt emission efficiency. The results require unusually low electron and magnetic energy fractions (εₑ, ε_B), implying that forward shock emission alone may be insufficient to explain the radiation without incorporating significant Synchrotron Self-Compton (SSC) components.

[12] Spin-up and spin distribution of stellar black holes grown by gas accretion in proto-stellar clusters

  • Authors: Zacharias Roupas
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18857v1
  • Methodology: [Simulation / Theory]
  • Core Results: A strong correlation exists between black hole mass and spin (a*) for BHs growing via gas accretion in compact clusters, with median spins reaching a* ~ 0.90 for mBH ≥ 100 M☉. Low-spin BHs (a* ≤ 0.3) are typically low-mass (< 25 M☉), while high-spin objects dominate the higher mass ranges, matching the parameters of GW signals like GW231123.

[13] UGC 2369S: a Kpc Scale Triple Merger Candidate Identified in a Nearby Luminous Infrared Galaxy

  • Authors: Yuanze Ding, Michael J. Koss, Fiona A. Harrison ★(Famous Scholar), Claudio Ricci ★(Famous Scholar), Ezequiel Treister ★(Famous Scholar)
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18499v1
  • Methodology: [Observation] - Energy band: X-ray (Chandra), NIR (AO), Radio, Optical - Key Targets/Sample: UGC 2369S (Triple merger)
  • Core Results: Multi-wavelength observations reveal a triple-nucleus merger with ~3 kpc separations, where the northern core harbors a heavily obscured AGN with NH ≳ 10²⁵ cm⁻² and MBH ≃ 10⁸ M☉. The other two cores show no evidence of current AGN activity and are likely metal-enriched remnants undergoing tidal stripping and cannibalization within the complex gravitational potential.

[18] Neutrinos and gamma rays from Seyfert galaxies constrain the properties of coronal turbulence

  • Authors: Federico Testagrossa, Damiano F. G. Fiorillo, Luca Comisso, Lorenzo Sironi ★(Famous Scholar)
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18214v1
  • Methodology: [Theory / Simulation]
  • Core Results: Multimessenger fits to NGC 1068 data favor a compact, strongly magnetized corona with a large turbulence coherence length. Conversely, NGC 7469 observations suggest a larger coronal size with a smaller coherence length and a significantly smaller fraction of energy allocated to non-thermal protons, providing quantitative limits on the turbulent magnetic field strength in AGN cores.

[21] Supermassive Black Hole Winds in X-rays: SUBWAYS V. Properties of hot coronae in quasars at intermediate redshift

  • Authors: S. Peluso, G. Lanzuisi, A. Comastri ★(Famous Scholar), M. Brusa ★(Famous Scholar), M. Giustini ★(Famous Scholar), G. Miniutti ★(Famous Scholar), S. Bianchi ★(Famous Scholar), E. Nardini ★(Famous Scholar), G. Ponti ★(Famous Scholar)
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18156v1
  • Methodology: [Observation] - Energy band: X-ray (XMM-Newton, NuSTAR) - Key Targets/Sample: 23 radio-quiet AGN (0.1 < z < 0.4)
  • Core Results: High-energy spectral analysis shows that hot coronae in luminous quasars (Lbol up to 2 × 10⁴⁶ erg/s) are cooler and optically thicker (median Ecut ≈ 87 keV, τ ≈ 1–5) than those in lower-luminosity Seyferts. A significant anti-correlation is detected between Ecut and both λEdd and Lbol, supporting a scenario where coronal cooling is enhanced by pair-production processes at high accretion rates.

[23] Spectral Hardness as the Primary Discriminator: Unveiling the Collapsar--Merger Boundary with a Gold-Standard Gamma-Ray Burst Sample

  • Authors: Xue Zhang, Yan-Kun Qu, et al., Fa-Yin Wang ★(Famous Scholar)
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18150v1
  • Methodology: [Methods / Machine Learning]
  • Core Results: Using a Support Vector Machine (SVM) on a gold-standard sample, this study demonstrates that spectral hardness (Eₚ,ᵢ) and energetics (Eᵢₛₒ) have five times the discriminative power of T₉₀,z in classifying GRB progenitors. The derived classification index I_SVM provides a physically motivated boundary that successfully distinguishes collapsars from mergers across different instrumental eras.
