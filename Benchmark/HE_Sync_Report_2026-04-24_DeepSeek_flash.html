[6] Delving into the depths of NGC 3783 with XRISM: V. Broad-band modeling of ionized outflows

  • Authors: Keqin Zhao, Jelle S. Kaastra ★(Famous Scholar), Liyi Gu, Missagh Mehdipour, Megan E. Eckart, Keigo Fukumura, Matteo Guainazzi ★(Famous Scholar), Chen Li, Christos Panagiotou, Matilde Signorini
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21710v1
  • Methodology: Observation (Joint spectral modeling of XMM-Newton/RGS and XRISM/Resolve time-averaged spectra)
    • Energy band: X-ray (0.3–10 keV)
    • Key Targets/Sample: Seyfert 1 galaxy NGC 3783
  • Core Results: The XRISM/Resolve spectrum reveals a multi-phase outflow with eight warm absorber components spanning ionization parameters log ξ = 1.08–3.38 and outflow velocities of 480–1230 km s-1. Total column density in 2024 is 1.5 times larger than in 2000–2001, with the dominant UTA absorber increasing its column density by a factor of three. These results demonstrate significant structural and dynamical evolution of the ionized outflows over 24 years.

[8] XRISM High-Resolution X-ray Spectroscopy of Cygnus X-1 — Orbital and Short-Term Variability of Iron Absorption

  • Authors: Kaito Ninoyu, Shinya Yamada, Natalie Hell, Elisa Costantini, Oluwashina Adegoke, Paul Draghis, Ken Ebisawa, Javier A. Garcia ★(Famous Scholar), Edmund Hodges-Kluck, Shunji Kitamoto, Shogo Kobayashi, Takayoshi Kohmura, Aya Kubota, Jon M. Miller ★(Famous Scholar), Misaki Mizumoto, Tsunefumi Mizuno, Hiromitsu Takahashi, Yuusuke Uchida, Kazutaka Yamaoka, Sixuan Zhang, Ryota Tomaru, Seoru Ito
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21557v1
  • Methodology: Observation (Orbital-phase-resolved and intensity-sorted spectroscopy with XRISM Resolve and Xtend)
    • Energy band: X-ray (Fe-K band, ~6–7 keV)
    • Key Targets/Sample: Black hole high-mass X-ray binary Cygnus X-1
  • Core Results: The Resolve microcalorimeter reveals orbital-phase-dependent variability in highly ionized Fe absorption lines, with column density, ionization state, and blueshifted velocity varying across the orbit. Tentative evidence of short-term (seconds) variability in Fe-K absorption during dip phases suggests clumpy, lower-ionization gas structures interacting with the X-ray source. These results constrain wind-fed accretion in the Cyg X-1 system.

[9] XRISM High-Resolution X-ray Spectroscopy of Cygnus X-1 — Highly Ionized Iron Absorption Structures

  • Authors: Shinya Yamada, Natalie Hell, Elisa Costantini, Oluwashina Adegoke, McKinley Brumback, Paul Draghis, Ken Ebisawa, Javier A. Garcia ★(Famous Scholar), Edmund Hodges-Kluck, Shunji Kitamoto, Shogo Kobayashi, Takayoshi Kohmura, Aya Kubota, Jon M. Miller ★(Famous Scholar), Misaki Mizumoto, Tsunefumi Mizuno, Kaito Ninoyu, Hiromitsu Takahashi, Yuusuke Uchida, Kazutaka Yamaoka, Sixuan Zhang
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21497v1
  • Methodology: Observation (High-resolution X-ray absorption line spectroscopy with XRISM Resolve)
    • Energy band: X-ray (Fe K-shell lines, ~6–7 keV)
    • Key Targets/Sample: Black hole X-ray binary Cygnus X-1
  • Core Results: Absorption from He-like and H-like Fe exhibits equivalent widths of 2.3 eV, 0.4 eV, and 1.2 eV, respectively, with a blueshifted velocity of ~100 km s-1 and an ionization parameter log ξ ~ 3 (NH ~ a few ×1021 cm-2). Strong absorption appears only during the dipping phase (φorb > 0.9), allowing the kinematics and spatial distribution of the wind in the vicinity of the black hole to be traced.

[11] Supermassive Black Hole Winds in X-rays: SUBWAYS IV. Tracing Radio Emission and Unveiling the Role of Winds

  • Authors: E. Amenta, M. Brienza, G. Bruni, M. Brusa, R. Morganti, F. Panessa, R. D. Baldi, E. Behar, G. Lanzuisi, T. Shimwell, F. Tombesi ★(Famous Scholar), S. Bianchi, G. Chartas, A. Comastri, G. Cresci, B. De Marco, F. Fiore, M. Gaspari ★(Famous Scholar), V. E. Gianolli, R. Gilli, S. B. Kraemer, G. Kriss, Y. Krongold, F. La Franca, A. L. Longinotti, M. Mehdipour, E. Nardini, M. Perna, P. Petrucci, E. Piconcelli, G. Ponti, F. Ricci, L. Zappacosta
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21405v1
  • Methodology: Observation (Multi-frequency radio SEDs from JVLA, LoTSS, and public surveys combined with X-ray properties)
    • Energy band: Radio (150 MHz – 6 GHz) and X-ray
    • Key Targets/Sample: 21 X-ray selected AGN from the SUBWAYS sample (log Lbol = 44.9–46.3 erg/s, z=0.1–0.5)
  • Core Results: Ultrafast outflows (UFOs) are detected in 30% of the sample; UFO hosts tend to show larger radio extension and steeper radio spectra, consistent with wind-driven shock models. The radio emission of the six UFO hosts aligns with predictions from wind-driven shocks, suggesting a direct physical connection between the X-ray UFO phase and larger-scale radio outflows.

[12] A Synchronized Spin Model for Black-Hole Accretion Systems

  • Authors: Masahiro Morikawa, Akika Nakamichi
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21390v1
  • Methodology: Theory (Analytical model of interacting macro-spins representing local dynamos in a rotating accretion flow)
  • Core Results: The Synchronized Spin Model links intermittent magnetic reconnection to broad-band X-ray variability, coronal heating, flares, and outflows. It reproduces 1/f‑like variability, rms–flux scaling, and approximately log‑normal flux distributions, and interprets the hard/soft cycle of X‑ray binaries as motion through magnetic state space. The model provides a unified picture for the coexistence of diverse phenomena in black‑hole accretion.
