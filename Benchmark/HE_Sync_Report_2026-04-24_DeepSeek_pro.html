[1] Testing solitonic boson star interpretations of Sagittarius A* with near-infrared flare astrometry

  • Authors: Xiangyu Wang, Zhenyu Zhang, Hai-Qing Zhang, Minyong Guo, Bin Chen
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21883v1
  • Methodology: Observation (GRAVITY NIR flare astrometry combined with χ2 and MCMC parameter fitting on five spherically symmetric solitonic boson-star models plus Schwarzschild black hole)
    • Energy band: Near-infrared
    • Key Targets/Sample: Sagittarius A*
  • Core Results: The inferred masses of boson-star models are systematically larger than the established value of 4.3×106 M☉, with more diffusive boson stars yielding mass estimates closer to this value. Both χ2 and MCMC methods produce consistent results, placing stringent constraints on solitonic boson star interpretations of Sgr A* without completely ruling them out. The imaging properties of more diffusive boson stars converge toward those of a Schwarzschild black hole.

[2] Mitigating Systematic Errors in Parameter Estimation of Binary Black Hole Mergers in O1-O3 LIGO-Virgo Data

  • Authors: Sumit Kumar, Max Melching, Frank Ohme, Harsh Narola, Tom Dooney, Chris Van Den Broeck
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21859v1
  • Methodology: Methods (Data-driven parametric models incorporating phase and amplitude uncertainties in GW waveforms, applied to selected LVK events)
  • Core Results: The proposed method effectively reduces systematic errors even from data artifacts such as glitches and the deglitching process. For GW191109_010717, anti-aligned spin characteristics persist but become consistent across raw and deglitched frame files and across IMRPhenomXPHM, IMRPhenomXO4a, and NRSur7dq4 waveform models. For GW200129_065458, the precession parameter converges to χp=0.60+0.31-0.33, 0.58+0.30-0.29, and 0.56+0.31-0.28 across the three waveform models respectively.

[3] An RXTE Search for the Sterile Neutrino Decay in Galaxy Clusters

  • Authors: Mark J. Henriksen
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21817v1
  • Methodology: Observation (Stacked spectral analysis of 3.1 Ms of RXTE archival cluster data, with Gaussian line modeling at 3.55 keV)
    • Energy band: X-ray (3.55 keV line)
    • Key Targets/Sample: Eight galaxy clusters including Coma, with individual excess emission
  • Core Results: A 2.5σ excess over a thermal model is found at the energy of the 3.55 keV sterile neutrino decay line, with a fitted line centered at 3.6 keV and model flux of 3.07×10-5 ph cm-2 s-1. The mixing angle for detected clusters ranges from 0.35 to 6.2×10-10, and the decay rate is strongly correlated with cluster temperature (r=0.87). Approximately half of the decay line flux originates from the Coma cluster, and low-temperature thermal emission plus systematics fail to fully account for the marginal excess.

[6] Delving into the depths of NGC 3783 with XRISM: V. Broad-band modeling of ionized outflows

  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21710v1
  • Methodology: Observation (Joint spectral modeling of XMM-Newton/RGS and XRISM/Resolve time-averaged spectra using the pion photoionization code within SPEX)
    • Energy band: X-ray
    • Key Targets/Sample: NGC 3783 (Seyfert 1 galaxy)
  • Core Results: Eight warm absorber components are identified spanning log ξ = 1.08–3.38 with outflow velocities of 480–1230 km s-1. B3) has increased its column density by a factor of three while maintaining a similar ionization parameter, indicating significant structural and dynamical evolution over 24 years.

  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21557v1
  • Methodology: Observation (First high-resolution X-ray spectroscopy of Cyg X-1 with XRISM/Resolve microcalorimeter and Xtend CCD imager, including orbital-phase-resolved and intensity-sorted analyses)
    • Energy band: X-ray (Fe-K band)
    • Key Targets/Sample: Cygnus X-1
  • Core Results: Highly ionized Fe absorption lines exhibit orbital-phase-dependent variability in column density, ionization state, and blueshifted velocity, suggesting structural variations in the focused stellar wind along the line of sight. [9] XRISM High-Resolution X-ray Spectroscopy of Cygnus X-1 -- highly ionized Iron absorption structures

  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21497v1
  • Methodology: Observation (High-resolution spectral analysis with XRISM/Resolve covering orbital phase 0.65–0.17 in the low/hard state)
    • Energy band: X-ray
    • Key Targets/Sample: Cygnus X-1
  • Core Results: The absorbing plasma is characterized by an ionization parameter of approximately 3, column densities of a few×1021 cm-2, and a blueshifted velocity of approximately 100 km s-1. These narrow absorption features of only a few eV demonstrate XRISM/Resolve's capability to trace wind kinematics and spatial distribution near the black hole.

[12] A Synchronized Spin Model for Black-Hole Accretion Systems

  • Authors: Masahiro Morikawa, Akika Nakamichi
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21390v1
  • Methodology: Theory (Proposal of a Synchronized Spin Model where multiple local dynamos in a rotating accretion flow are represented as interacting macro-spins, with collective variables organizing timing statistics and large-scale morphology)
  • Core Results: Multiscale magnetic reconnection sustains coronal heating, flares, intermittent outflows, and discrete jet activity, while synchronization dynamics produce amplitude modulation and demodulation providing a route to 1/f-like variability, rms–flux/Taylor-like scaling, and approximately log-normal statistics. The model interprets the hard/soft cycle of X-ray binaries as motion through magnetic state space, connecting broad-band X-ray variability, hot coronae, wide-angle winds, and both steady and discrete jets into a unified framework.

[17] An Analytic Threshold for LESA-Driven Negative ELN Flux Directions in Core-Collapse Supernovae: Derivation and Population Census

  • Authors: Nicolás Viaux, Lucas Johns
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21081v1
  • Methodology: Simulation (Derivation of an analytic diagnostic tested against 33 independent 3D CCSN simulations: 25 Princeton/Fornax models spanning 8.1–100 M☉ and 8 Garching models including rotating cases)
  • Core Results: Of 23 non-black-hole-forming Princeton models, 22 cross the LESA threshold with median onset tc=225 ms (IQR 162–264 ms, CV=18.6%). The threshold correctly identifies the anti-LESA-pole transition, distinguishing the global LESA-driven crossing from early localized turbulent crossings. The fast-rotating Garching 15 M☉ model is correctly classified as a non-crosser, and both BH-forming models cross near 250 ms post-bounce and remain above threshold for 1807 and 2463 ms before collapse. The emitted ν̄e energy flux may exceed the νe flux along some lines of sight in the next nearby CCSN.

[19] Archival Multiband Gravitational-Wave Signals from Massive Black Hole Binary Mergers

  • Authors: Alexander W. Criswell, Stephen R. Taylor ★(Famous Scholar), Kris Pardo, Alberto Sesana, David Izquierdo, Silvia Bonoli, Daniele Spinoso
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21013v1
  • Methodology: Theory (Analytical and numerical investigation of orphaned low-frequency signals in PTA pulsar terms, with stacking methodology for multiband search prospects with LISA and astrometric data)
  • Core Results: MBHBs merging at GW frequencies above the PTA sensitivity band leave orphaned low-frequency contributions in the PTA pulsar term, which acts as a time-delayed probe of a chirping merger with frequency response determined by sky direction and intrinsic binary properties. An approach to stack these orphaned pulsar terms across the array is demonstrated, opening prospects for an archival multiband search in conjunction with LISA and astrometric observations.

[20] Gravity Echoes from Supermassive Black Hole Binaries

  • Authors: Qinyuan Zheng, Bence Bécsy, Chiara M. F. Mingarelli ★(Famous Scholar)
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21010v1
  • Methodology: Theory (Analytical framework demonstrating that μHz-band detection of a nearby massive binary by a future mission such as μAres turns PTA pulsar terms into targeted probes of binary evolution, analogous to supernova light echoes)
  • Core Results: For a fiducial equal-mass binary with total mass 109 M☉ at 80 Mpc, the combined PTA echo SNR reaches 33, with up to 24 pulsars individually resolving the signal among pulsars with 50-year baselines. Single-pulsar echo sensitivity alone enables independent sky localization to ~10–100 deg2, and resolved pulsar-term frequencies directly measure the binary inspiral rate from hundreds to thousands of years ago. A small set of anchor pulsars could phase-connect the array and trace post-Newtonian evolution coherently over kpc baselines.

[21] Probing Supermassive Black Hole Mergers with Pulsar Timing Arrays

  • Authors: Hippolyte Quelquejay Leclere
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20975v1
  • Methodology: Theory (SMBHB population modeling demonstrating that the pulsar term in timing residual waveforms enables PTAs to probe SMBHBs that merged before observations began, termed "zombie binaries")
  • Core Results: While the probability of detecting zombie binaries in existing PTA datasets remains low, the Square Kilometer Array observatory is expected to achieve sufficient sensitivity to detect a few zombie binaries with SNR exceeding 3. These systems represent a new class of PTA sources that opens a novel window for studying the most massive SMBHBs in the local universe.

[22] Chaotic migration of LISA Extreme Mass Ratio Inspirals in a turbulent accretion disk: effect on waveform de-phasing

  • Authors: Mudit Garg, Lucio Mayer ★(Famous Scholar), Yinhao Wu, Yacine Ali-Haïmoud, Douglas N. C. Lin
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20971v1
  • Methodology: Simulation (Proof-of-concept prescription for turbulent torque Tturb modeled as a Gaussian distribution around the linear torque Tlin, applied to a "golden" circular EMRI with M=106 M☉, q=5×10-5, at z=0.276 with SNR=50)
  • Core Results: For fEdd≳0.3, turbulence normalization C≳300, disk aspect ratio h0≳0.03, and viscous coefficient α≳0.1, gas-induced dephasings become detectable (Δψgas>8/SNR) when considering chaotic migration due to turbulent gas flow, whereas they would be unobservable if only linear torques were considered. This result motivates running MHD simulations of accretion disks with embedded LISA EMRIs to understand the evolution of their orbital elements and the imprint of turbulent environments on GW waveforms.

[23] Turbulence Mode Decomposition and Anisotropy in Magnetically Dominated Collisionless Plasmas

  • Authors: Samuel T. Sebastian, Siyao Xu, Yue Hu, Luca Comisso, Saikat Das, Joonas Nättilä
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20963v1
  • Methodology: Simulation (3D fully kinetic simulation extending the Cho & Lazarian MHD turbulence mode decomposition method to the collisionless plasma regime)
  • Core Results: Alfvén and slow modes are anisotropic following Goldreich–Sridhar scaling, while fast modes are isotropic. A larger kinetic energy fraction of fast modes is observed compared to non-relativistic MHD turbulence, suggesting stronger Alfvén–fast mode coupling in relativistic magnetized collisionless plasmas. Dominant thermal fluctuations in the kinetic range cause flattening of the turbulent velocity structure function and weakening of turbulence anisotropy and dynamic alignment near kinetic scales.
