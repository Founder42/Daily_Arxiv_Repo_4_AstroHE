[1] Testing solitonic boson star interpretations of Sagittarius A* with near-infrared flare astrometry

  • Authors: Xiangyu Wang, Zhenyu Zhang, Hai-Qing Zhang, Minyong Guo, Bin Chen
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21883v1
  • Methodology: Observation (Near-infrared flare astrometry and orbital parameter fitting)
    • Energy band: Infrared
    • Key Targets/Sample: Sagittarius A*
  • Core Results: Inferred masses for solitonic boson-star models are systematically larger than the established value of 4.3 × 106 M☉. While more diffusive boson stars mimic black hole imaging properties, the centroid motions and image reconstructions provide stringent constraints that favor the Schwarzschild black hole interpretation.

[3] An RXTE Search for the Sterile Neutrino Decay in Galaxy Clusters

  • Authors: Mark J. Henriksen
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21817v1
  • Methodology: Observation (Archival spectral analysis of cluster data)
    • Energy band: X-ray
    • Key Targets/Sample: Eight galaxy clusters (including Coma)
  • Core Results: A 2.5σ excess of emission is identified over a thermal model at 3.6 keV with a model flux of 3.07 × 10-5 ph cm-2 s-1. The decay rate inferred from the line flux is strongly correlated (r = 0.87) with cluster temperature, suggesting that hotter, more massive clusters host larger dark matter reservoirs.

[4] Magnetar Engines in Broad-lined Type Ic Supernovae and a Unified Picture for Magnetar-powered Stripped-envelope Supernovae

  • Authors: Jin-Ping Zhu, Bing Zhang
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21759v1
  • Methodology: Theory (Magnetar engine modeling of multi-band lightcurves)
  • Core Results: Modeling of 80 SNe Ic-BL indicates magnetar parameters of Pi ∼ 2.04 ms and Bp ∼ 3.96 × 1015 G. A universal Mej–Pi anti-correlation is confirmed across SNe Ic-BL, SLSNe Ic, and FBOTs, supporting a common origin in magnetar-powered engines.

[5] Gauging the Impact of Cosmic Ray Feedback on the Stellar Initial Mass Function

  • Authors: Margot Fitz Axen, Stella S. R. Offner, Philip F. Hopkins, Michael Y. Grudić
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21754v1
  • Methodology: Simulation (Magnetohydrodynamic simulations with cosmic ray transport)
  • Core Results: Cosmic ray feedback increases the star formation efficiency in molecular clouds by 43% by compressing gas into higher density structures. The resulting Initial Mass Function (IMF) becomes significantly more top-heavy, with a high-mass slope shallower by ∼20% compared to non-cosmic-ray simulations.

  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21710v1
  • Methodology: Observation (High-resolution X-ray spectroscopy)
    • Energy band: X-ray (XRISM/Resolve and XMM-Newton/RGS)
    • Key Targets/Sample: NGC 3783

The total column density in 2024 is 1.5 times larger than that observed in 2000-2001, indicating significant structural evolution and the replenishment of outflowing material over a 24-year baseline.

  • Authors: Masahiro Morikawa, Akika Nakamichi
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21390v1
  • Methodology: Theory (Dynamical system modeling of magnetic reconnection)
  • Core Results: The Synchronized Spin Model (SSM) interprets X-ray variability and state transitions as the synchronization of local magnetic dynamos. This framework naturally reproduces 1/f-like variability and log-normal statistics by modeling coronal heating and discrete jet activity as emergent properties of interacting macro-spins.

[15] Geometry, Not Calorimetry, Drives the Radio/Infrared/Gamma-Ray Correlation

  • Authors: Troy A. Porter, Igor V. Moskalenko, Gudlaugur Johannesson
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21224v1
  • Methodology: Simulation (GALPROP 3D modeling of cosmic ray propagation)
  • Core Results: Tight radio-IR-γ-ray correlations arise primarily from geometric line-of-sight integration through radially-structured galactic discs rather than local calorimetry. This implies the correlation scatter is a diagnostic of galactic structure and viewing angle rather than stochastic physical noise.

[19] Archival Multiband Gravitational-Wave Signals from Massive Black Hole Binary Mergers

  • Authors: Alexander W. Criswell, Stephen R. Taylor, Kris Pardo, Alberto Sesana, David Izquierdo, Silvia Bonoli, Daniele Spinoso
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21013v1
  • Methodology: Theory (PTA signal modeling)
  • Core Results: Massive black hole binaries leave "orphaned" low-frequency contributions in the pulsar term of PTA data even after the merger has passed the sensitivity band. These archival signals allow for multiband GW searches that link pulsar timing observations to future LISA detections.

[20] Gravity Echoes from Supermassive Black Hole Binaries

  • Authors: Qinyuan Zheng, Bence Bécsy, Chiara M. F. Mingarelli
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21010v1
  • Methodology: Methods (Sensitivity analysis for μHz-nHz detectors)
  • Core Results: PTA pulsar terms act as "gravity echoes," providing snapshots of SMBH binaries hundreds of years before the merger. For a 109 M☉ binary at 80 Mpc, the combined echo signal-to-noise ratio reaches 33, enabling sky localization to ∼10-100 deg2.

[21] Probing Supermassive Black Hole Mergers with Pulsar Timing Arrays

  • Authors: Hippolyte Quelquejay Leclere
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20975v1
  • Methodology: Theory (Population modeling of "zombie binaries")
  • Core Results: PTAs can detect "zombie binaries" — SMBHs that merged before timing observations began — via the pulsar term. The Square Kilometer Array is predicted to detect several such systems with SNR > 3, opening a new observational window into the local universe's merger history.

[22] Chaotic migration of LISA Extreme Mass Ratio Inspirals in a turbulent accretion disk: effect on waveform de-phasing

  • Authors: Mudit Garg, Lucio Mayer, Yinhao Wu, Yacine Ali-Haïmoud, Douglas N. C. Lin
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20971v1
  • Methodology: Simulation (Hydrodynamical de-phasing modeling)
  • Core Results: Gas-induced de-phasing of EMRI waveforms becomes detectable by LISA (Δψgas > 8/SNR) when accounting for chaotic migration in turbulent disks (fEdd ≳ 0.3). Turbulent torques significantly alter the GW-driven trajectory compared to laminar disk models, potentially providing a diagnostic of the AGN disk environment.

[25] Revisiting radio synchrotron diagnostics in star-forming galaxies

  • Authors: Maria Werhahn, Christoph Pfrommer, Philipp Girichidis, Joseph Whittingham, Léna Jlassi, Rüdiger Pakmor, Rebekka Bieri, Rainer Weinberger, Volker Springel, Freeke van de Voort
  • Link: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20950v1
  • Methodology: Simulation (MHD with Lagrangian tracer particles)
  • Core Results: Advection-only transport fails to reproduce extended vertical radio haloes, suggesting that additional re-acceleration or transport mechanisms are required in galactic winds.
